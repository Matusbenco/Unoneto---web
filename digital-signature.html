How does digital signing work?

1

Send documents for signature

Simply upload PDF documents for signing to the DigiSign app and attach files. Enter the list of signers - name, phone, email, and identity verification method. Assign cover messages.

2

DigiSign secures the electronic signature

The system automatically sends documents to the signers. Identity verification follows (SMS code, BankID, eID card, personal documents). After verification, the signer can sign the document. The entire process is securely monitored.

3

Everyone receives the signed document

Signed documents are immediately available to all parties. They include a transaction ID, signature marks, audit trail (complete history), electronic seal, and timestamp. Document retrieval is secured by eDelivery.

4

Secure Storage

All documents are securely archived in encrypted cloud storage with the option of re-stamping. You have access to them anytime, anywhere.

Supported Signature Types

DigiSign supports all types of electronic signatures according to the eIDAS regulation, from simple to qualified electronic signatures.

Simple Electronic Signature (EES)

For common documents where identity confirmation via email or SMS is sufficient. Ideal for internal documents, orders, or preliminary agreements.

Examples: Orders, reservations, data processing consent

Advanced Electronic Signature (AES)

With a higher level of identity verification. Used for more important documents where stronger verification of the signer's identity is needed.

Examples: Employment contracts, business contracts, mandate agreements

Qualified Electronic Signature (QES)

The highest level of security. Equivalent to a handwritten signature. Uses an eID card with a certificate or Bank Identity.

Examples: Lease agreements, loan agreements, legal documents

Frequently Asked Questions

Is an electronic signature really legally valid?

Yes! An electronic signature is fully recognized in the Slovak Republic and throughout the European Union. Its validity is governed by the eIDAS regulation (EU 910/2014). A qualified electronic signature even has the same legal force as a handwritten signature. DigiSign supports all levels of electronic signatures.

What documents can I sign electronically?

You can sign almost all types of documents electronically—employment contracts, business contracts, invoices, orders, consents, mandate agreements, rental agreements, and many more. The only exceptions are special documents that the law explicitly requires to be signed by hand (e.g., a will, a prenuptial agreement).

Do my clients need any special application?

No! That's the beauty of DigiSign. Your clients don't need to install anything. All they need is an email and a phone. For a qualified signature, they will need an eID card with a reader or use Bank Identity, but even in this case, nothing needs to be installed.

How quickly can I integrate DigiSign into my system?

You can implement a basic integration via API within a few hours to days, depending on the complexity of your system. DigiSign has a fully API-driven architecture and comprehensive documentation. We will gladly help you with the integration and significantly speed up the process.

What is Bank Identity and how does it work?

Bank Identity is a method of verifying identity through your internet banking. The client logs into their bank and confirms their identity—all online, without needing an in-person visit or special device. DigiSign is a partner of Bank Identity and can provide you with access to it.

Where are the signed documents stored?

All documents are securely stored in DigiSign's encrypted cloud storage. You have access to them anytime, anywhere. Documents can be downloaded at any time and archived in your own systems as well. DigiSign meets strict security standards and GDPR requirements.
